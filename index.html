Fushimi Inari Shrine 1.5–2 hrs
Thousands of orange torii gates winding up a forested mountain. Go early — ideally before 8am. The lower trail to Yotsutsuji viewpoint takes 30 min up and is the most iconic section. You don't need to go all the way to the top.
⛩️ Fun fact: There are over 10,000 torii gates on this mountain, donated by businesses and individuals over centuries. Each one has the donor's name and donation date carved on the back.

Nishiki Market 45 min
"Kyoto's kitchen" — a 400m covered market alley. Try tamagoyaki (sweet rolled omelette), fresh tofu, pickled vegetables, and wagashi sweets. Halal-safe snacking: stick to egg, tofu, and fresh fruit stalls.
🐡 Fun fact: Nishiki Market has existed on this exact spot for over 400 years. In the Edo period it was exclusively a fish market — the name means "brocade" because the stalls were arranged in colourful rows.

Ryōan-ji · Rock Garden 45 min
Japan's most famous rock garden — 15 stones arranged so you can never see all at once. Sit on the wooden platform and stare at it for 10 minutes. That's the whole point.
🪨 Fun fact: Nobody knows who designed the garden or what it means. Theories include islands in the ocean, a tiger carrying cubs, and a Zen mind puzzle. The mystery is deliberate.

Kinkaku-ji · The Golden Pavilion 45 min
Gold-leaf pavilion reflected in the mirror pond. It's genuinely magnificent — but very busy. Accept the crowd, take the photo, spend 30 minutes circling the pond. Early arrival has shorter queues.
